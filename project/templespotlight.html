Temple Spotlight: Oakland, California

Historic Details

Located along the San Fransisco Bay, the Oakland, California Temple was announced on 23 January 1961 and its groundbreaking followed on 26 May 1962. When it was originally dedicated on 17 November 1964, it was the 13th temple of The Church and the second in California. (Los Angeles was the first temple in California.)

Early members of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints sailed to San Francisco, which was then known as Yerba Buena, from New York City on the Ship Brooklyn, arriving in July 1846. There were 238 men, women, and children, and they soon established the first school, library, bank, and newspaper in California. In 1847 and again in 1924, prophets of The Church foresaw a temple in the East Bay Hills. In 1942, President David O. McKay visited the site where the Oakland Temple now sits and authorized the purchase of the land.

On 16 June 2019, the Oakland Temple was rededicated after extensive renovations. For photos taken between these renovations and the rededication, see this news page on churchofjesuschrist.org .

The Oakland Temple has beautiful grounds where visitors can wander and offers a stunning view of the Bay Area. For more information and history, visit OaklandTemple.org.

Visitors' Center

Open 9am through 9pm daily, the Oakland Temple Visitors' Center welcomes visitors and members alike. The large front windows reveal a large statue of the Christus, His arms open wide in love for all. Come in and talk to the missionaries, enjoy the exhibits, or just rest and bask in the peace and tranquility.

The lower level of the Visitors' Center houses the Oakland California FamilySearch Library. Hours are Tuesday-Wednesday: 10am-9pm; Thursday-Saturday: 10am-4pm; Sunday: 3-7pm; (closed Monday).
